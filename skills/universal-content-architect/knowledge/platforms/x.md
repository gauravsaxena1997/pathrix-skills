# X (Twitter)

Fast, opinionated, reply-driven. The platform rewards sharp takes and punchy writing. Zero tolerance for fluff.

## Format hierarchy (what works)

1. **Contrarian single tweet with data** : "X is overrated. Here's why [number]."
2. **Threads (5 to 10 tweets)** : build momentum, each tweet earns the next
3. **Personal lesson tweet** : "I just learned X. Sharing so you don't waste Y hours."
4. **Meme with context** : niche-specific (dev culture, startup life, freelancing)
5. **Poll + reveal** : poll first, then reply with your take

## Thread structure

- **Tweet 1 (hook)** : promise a specific insight or outcome. Clear stakes.
- **Tweets 2 to N-1 (build)** : each tweet earns the next. No filler.
- **Final tweet (payoff)** : deliver the promise. End with one-line CTA (follow, reply, link).
- **Self-reply game** : reply to your own tweets with examples, data, screenshots. Extends reach.

## Pacing rules

- Each tweet must make reader want the next
- Shorter tweets > longer ones; break thoughts into separate tweets
- No "1/" numbering at end of each tweet (outdated, feels spammy)
- Line breaks for readability

## What works on X specifically

- **Contrarian takes with data to back them**
- **Thread-form wisdom** : numbered lists, numbered insights
- **Niche memes** : dev culture, startup pain, freelance absurdity
- **"I learned X by..." personal insights**
- **Poll + insight combo**
- **Live-tweeting builds** : ship updates in real time
- **Reply to viral tweets in your niche** : free visibility if your reply is sharp

## Posting cadence (Gaurav : 1 per week)

For low-volume cadence:
- Make each tweet count
- Favor threads over single tweets (higher reach ceiling)
- Reply game matters more than posting when posting is rare
- 10 to 20 quality replies per day on niche accounts > 1 post per week alone

## Reply strategy (underrated)

X's algorithm rewards quality replies to larger accounts in your niche. This is the fastest organic growth vector for low-posting accounts.

Tactics:
- Find 10 to 15 niche accounts > 10k followers
- Reply within 15 minutes of their new post
- Add value: counter-take, data point, extension, or useful joke
- Never "Great post!" replies
- Aim for replies that the original poster wants to engage with

## Character economy

- 280 character limit per tweet
- Use sparingly : best tweets are under 200 characters
- No hashtags in main content (they read as amateur on X)
- Mentions only when relevant

## Hook formulas that work on X

- "Unpopular opinion: [specific take]"
- "3 things about [niche] I wish I knew 2 years ago:"
- "I've been doing [thing] wrong for 5 years. Here's what I learned:"
- "[Product] vs [product]. I've used both. Here's my actual take:"
- "If you're [role], save this thread:"

## Anti-patterns

- Threads without momentum (each tweet must pull the next)
- "Follow for more" at end of every post
- Engagement bait ("like and retweet if you agree")
- Stale takes (X rewards speed)
- Rehashing the same take repeatedly
- Hashtags (outdated on X)
- Over-tagging influencers
- Vague openers ("just wanted to share...")

## Repurposing from long content

- **Blog post** : extract 5 to 7 key claims as a thread
- **Video** : pull 3 to 5 timestamps as separate tweets over a week
- **Mini project** : build-in-public thread with screenshots
- **Reddit post** : reformat TL;DR as single tweet, link profile for deep dive
