# Hooks & Psychology

The first 0.5 seconds decide whether scroll continues. The last 10 seconds decide whether the algorithm shows it to 10x more people.

## Hook formulas (proven)

1. **Result + timeframe + pain removed**
   Example: "Got 1,000 followers in 30 days without posting daily."
   Why it works: promise minus friction = urgency.

2. **Contradiction of belief**
   Example: "If you still think ab workouts burn belly fat, you're wasting effort."
   Why: disrupts pattern recognition, creates cognitive tension.

3. **Pattern interrupt (odd or punchy)**
   Example: "Stop using ChatGPT like a boomer."
   Why: novelty captures attention.

4. **Story frame (relatability)**
   Example: "I was [relatable situation] until [transformation]."
   Why: mirrors audience experience, builds trust.

5. **Mistake reversal**
   Example: "Stop doing X. Here's what to do instead."
   Why: embarrassment avoidance + solution hope.

6. **Insider secret**
   Example: "3 things [authority figures] never tell you about X."
   Why: scarcity + insider status + curiosity gap.

7. **Versus / comparison**
   Example: "Next.js vs Remix: which I'd actually pick in 2026."
   Why: debate instinct, engagement trigger.

8. **Specific result proof**
   Example: "This one change cut my bounce rate from 74% to 28%."
   Why: specificity = believability.

9. **Identity anchor**
   Example: "If you're an introverted founder, read this."
   Why: audience segmentation + belonging.

10. **Direct contradiction with stakes**
    Example: "Everyone told me my landing page looked fine. I almost believed them."
    Why: stakes + vulnerability = watch-to-resolution.

11. **List promise with odd number**
    Example: "7 things to stop doing in 2026."
    Why: scannability + completion satisfaction. Odd numbers > even.

12. **Tutorial / how-to**
    Example: "Here's exactly how to ship a Next.js app in 30 minutes."
    Why: promised clarity + actionability.

## Caption structure (IG, LinkedIn, Threads)

**First 125 characters (pre-More cutoff):**
- Lead with hook or curiosity gap, not context
- Zero fluff openers (no "Hey everyone", no "In today's post")
- Make the promise specific

**Body:**
- 2-3 sentence paragraphs max
- Problem -> agitation -> solution arc
- Specificity (numbers, names, dates) anchors credibility
- End paragraphs with micro-questions when engagement is the goal

**CTA (1-2 sentences):**
- Match CTA to funnel position (see 02-funnels-tofu-mofu-bofu.md)
- Conversational, not shouty ("save this for when you need it" > "SAVE NOW")
- One CTA per post, never two

## CTA patterns by funnel stage

| Stage | CTA Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness (TOFU) | Share | "Send this to a friend who needs it." |
| Trust (MOFU) | Save | "Save this for when you actually build it." |
| Lead gen | Comment keyword | "Comment GUIDE and I'll DM the full breakdown." |
| Conversion (BOFU) | Link/DM | "Link in bio for the free trial." |

Low-friction actions win. Save > Share > Comment > Link. Never ask for follow alone.

## Scroll-stop psychology (visual layer)

What makes thumbs pause:

- **0 to 0.5 seconds:** text overlay appears on-screen (not voiceover)
- **Pattern interrupt:** zoom, jump cut, color shift
- **Direct eye contact + bold statement:** breaks infinite scroll
- **Audio switch at start:** changes expectation mid-feed
- **Starting mid-action or mid-sentence:** unresolved tension
- **Specificity in hook:** "74% bounce rate" > "bad bounce rate"

**Key insight:** first 3 seconds are overrated. Completion rate and rewatches matter more. Build to a payoff in the last 10 seconds. End with a phrase or visual that mirrors the start to trigger a rewatch.

## Hook anti-patterns (never do)

- Clickbait with no payoff
- "You won't believe what happened next"
- Hyped adjectives with no proof ("incredible", "mind-blowing")
- Vague promise ("some tips on productivity")
- Asking a question without a reason to stay
- Starting with context instead of tension

## Editing pass for hooks

Before publishing, ask:
1. Does the first line promise a specific outcome?
2. Would a stranger scrolling at 3x speed stop?
3. Is there a reason to watch to the end?
4. Does the last beat reward or resolve the opening?
5. Can a 12-year-old repeat the promise after reading once?

If any answer is no, rewrite.
